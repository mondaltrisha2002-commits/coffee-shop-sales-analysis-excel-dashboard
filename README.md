# Coffee Shop Sales Analysis Dashboard

## Introduction

This project presents an interactive **Coffee Shop Sales Analysis Dashboard** built entirely in **Microsoft Excel** using **Power Query, Power Pivot, Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, and Excel Dashboarding techniques**. The objective of this project is to transform raw transactional sales data into meaningful business insights that support informed decision-making.

The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of overall sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, store-wise performance, product category contribution, peak business hours, and top-selling products. Interactive slicers allow users to analyze sales by **Month** and **Day**, making the report dynamic and user-friendly.

---

## Problem Statement

To design an interactive Excel dashboard that analyzes coffee shop sales data and uncovers valuable business insights related to sales performance, customer footfall, purchasing patterns, product popularity, store performance, and operational trends, enabling stakeholders to make data-driven business decisions.

---

## Sales Analysis Objectives

### 1. Overall Business Performance
- Calculate Total Sales generated across all stores.
- Measure Total Customer Footfall.
- Calculate Average Bill per Customer.
- Calculate Average Orders per Customer.

### 2. Hourly Sales Trend
- Analyze customer ordering behavior throughout the day.
- Identify peak business hours with the highest order volume.
- Understand how customer demand changes over operating hours.

### 3. Product Category Performance
- Analyze sales contribution of each product category.
- Identify the highest revenue-generating categories.
- Compare category-wise sales distribution.

### 4. Beverage Size Analysis
- Analyze customer preferences for beverage sizes.
- Compare demand across Large, Regular, Small, and Undefined sizes.

### 5. Store Performance Analysis
- Compare sales and customer footfall across different store locations.
- Identify stores with the highest customer traffic and revenue contribution.

### 6. Product Performance Analysis
- Identify the Top 5 products based on sales revenue.
- Evaluate products contributing most to overall business performance.

### 7. Weekday Order Analysis
- Analyze customer ordering patterns throughout the week.
- Identify the busiest and slowest business days.

---

## Sales Analysis Reports

## 1. Overall Business Performance

### Insights
- Total Sales reached **$698,812.33** across all coffee shop locations.
- The stores served **149,116 customers**, reflecting strong customer engagement.
- Customers spent an average of **$4.69 per bill**.
- Each customer placed an average of **1.44 orders**, indicating that most purchases consisted of single items with occasional add-on products.

---

## 2. Quantity Orders Based on Hours

### Insights
- Customer demand increases significantly during the early morning.
- The highest number of orders is recorded between **8 AM and 10 AM**, with **10 AM** being the busiest hour.
- Order volume gradually declines after the morning rush and continues to decrease during evening hours.
- These insights help identify the most important operating hours for staffing and inventory planning.

---

## 3. Category-wise Sales Distribution

### Insights
- **Coffee** contributes the largest share of revenue, accounting for **39%** of total sales.
- **Bakery products** generate **12%** of total sales, making them the second-largest contributor.
- **Drinking Chocolate** contributes **10%**, while **Coffee Beans** account for **6%**.
- Categories such as **Branded Products**, **Flavours**, and **Loose Tea** contribute comparatively smaller portions of overall revenue.

---

## 4. Beverage Size Distribution

### Insights
- **Regular-sized beverages** represent the highest percentage of customer orders at **31%**.
- **Large** and **Not Defined** beverage sizes each contribute **30%** of total orders.
- **Small-sized beverages** account for only **9%**, indicating relatively lower customer preference.

---

## 5. Store Location Performance

### Insights
- **Hell's Kitchen** records the highest customer footfall among all locations.
- **Astoria** follows closely with consistently strong sales and customer traffic.
- **Lower Manhattan** performs slightly behind the other two stores while maintaining stable revenue generation.
- Overall, all three locations demonstrate balanced business performance with only minor variations in sales and footfall.

---

## 6. Top 5 Products Based on Sales

### Insights
The highest revenue-generating products include:

- **Barista Espresso** — **$91,406.20**
- **Brewed Chai Tea** — **$77,081.95**
- **Hot Chocolate** — **$72,416.00**
- **Gourmet Brewed Coffee** — **$70,034.60**
- **Brewed Black Tea** — **$47,932.00**

These products contribute a substantial share of total revenue and represent the coffee shop's strongest-performing menu items.

---

## 7. Orders Across Weekdays

### Insights
- **Friday** records the highest number of customer orders.
- **Thursday** and **Monday** also experience consistently high customer traffic.
- **Saturday** has the lowest number of orders compared to the rest of the week.
- Customer demand remains relatively stable throughout weekdays, indicating consistent purchasing behavior.

## Key Business Insights

- The coffee shop generated nearly **$699K** in revenue while serving over **149K customers**, demonstrating healthy overall business performance.
- Customer demand is concentrated during the **morning hours (8 AM–10 AM)**, making this the busiest period for operations.
- **Coffee** remains the primary revenue driver, contributing **39%** of total sales, followed by Bakery products.
- **Regular-sized beverages** are the most preferred choice among customers, accounting for the largest share of total orders.
- **Hell's Kitchen** attracts the highest customer footfall, while all three store locations maintain consistent sales performance.
- **Barista Espresso** is the highest revenue-generating product, followed by Brewed Chai Tea and Hot Chocolate.
- Order volume remains steady throughout the workweek, with **Friday** recording the highest demand and **Saturday** the lowest.
